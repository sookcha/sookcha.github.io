A short novel that contains more truth about shame, desire, and cowardice than most novels three times its length. David is one of the great self-deceiving narrators — you know what he cannot admit long before he does.

Baldwin writes Paris as a city of moral consequence, not atmosphere. The room of the title becomes a symbol so earned it never feels like a symbol.
