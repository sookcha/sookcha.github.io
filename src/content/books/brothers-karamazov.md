The Grand Inquisitor chapter is the greatest single chapter in the Western novel. It contains the most honest argument against God that God's most devoted servant ever wrote.

Dostoevsky stacks the deck against faith deliberately — Alyosha is the weakest debater in the room — and the faith survives anyway, not through argument but through action. That is the whole point.
