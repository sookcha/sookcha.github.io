A man looking for his cat ends up at the bottom of a dry well confronting the violence of twentieth-century Japan. The non-sequitur is the logic.

Murakami's genius is that his surrealism never feels arbitrary — every strange thing that happens is emotionally necessary even when it is narratively inexplicable. The well is real. What happens in it is real. The cat is real.
