One day in London. Two people who never meet. Woolf makes consciousness the landscape — you move through Clarissa's mind the way you move through a city, following small attractions and sudden turns.

Septimus carries what Clarissa cannot afford to carry. His death frees her for a moment into clarity. The novel does not judge this arrangement but it does not let you miss it either.
