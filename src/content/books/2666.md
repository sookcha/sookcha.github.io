The Part About the Crimes is three hundred pages of dead women catalogued in forensic prose. It is the most morally serious piece of writing of the twenty-first century. You are required to not look away.

Bolaño builds an architecture of omission. The killer, the city, the system behind the killings — none are ever fully named. The horror is in the bureaucratic patience of the sentences.
