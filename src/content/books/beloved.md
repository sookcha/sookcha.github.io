Morrison writes about the unspeakable by moving around it. The horror of slavery is never summarized, only accumulated, detail by detail, until the weight becomes unbearable.

The ghost is not a metaphor. That is the hardest thing to accept and the most important. Sethe's love is not a metaphor either. The novel refuses to let you read it as anything but literal.
