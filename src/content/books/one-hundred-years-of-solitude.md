The first sentence is one of the greatest opening lines in literature. García Márquez understood that the past is not past — it circles back, it repeats, it haunts with different names.

Magic realism here is not whimsy. The yellow butterflies, the rain of flowers, the levitation — these are just the literal texture of a world where memory and history are indistinguishable from myth.
